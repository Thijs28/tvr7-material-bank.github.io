1. Pre-Demolition Mass
(Oorspronkelijk Volume)

The TVR7 complex is a dense, physical material bank. Prior to intervention, the structural BIM model registers exactly 6,484.19 m³ of pure solid mass, representing a massive consolidation of raw material waiting for phase-shifted extraction.

2. Selective Deconstruction
(Selectieve Ontmanteling)

High-yield assets are isolated: Brickwork (baksteen), blue stone flooring (blauwe steen), and wood wainscoting are extracted completely intact, entering the secondary market to offset overarching costs.

3. The Permanent Skeleton
(Permanent Skelet)

Following the harvest, the subtractive sequence concludes. Over 3,760 m³ of physical mass is demolished and removed, leaving exactly 2,723.36 m³ of stripped, load-bearing concrete framework for the next architectural iteration.

Macroscopic Volume Breakdown (Volumeverdeling)

A true physical representation of the BIM model data. The total pre-demolition physical mass (6,484.19 m³) is split into the retained post-demolition structure and the demolished mass. The demolished mass is further subdivided to highlight the immense proportional difference between salvaged materials (1.06%) and bulk waste/recycling (98.94%).

Material Flow Analysis (Materiaalstroomanalyse)

An exhaustive mapping of all extracted materials from the TVR7 site to their final economic or ecological destinations. Due to conflicting volume metrics in the contractor's data (m³, m², m, st), visual line width represents abstract mass proportion. Hover over flows for exact units.

The retained structural core (2,723 m³) locks into place and turns black. The demolished volume (3,761 m³) is severed from the geometry, exploding into a chaotic point cloud.
